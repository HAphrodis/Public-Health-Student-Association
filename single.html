ISHEMA RYANJYE PROJECT

SRHR program addresses issues related to comprehensive sexuality education by increasing awareness on Sexual and Reproductive Health and Rights (SRHR), promoting access to accurate information to the community specifically adolescents related to SRHR, family planning (FP), Gender-Based Violence (GBV), Gender Promotion (GP) and sexually transmitted infections (STIs) including HIV. Our aim is to crack fears of adolescents as well as children and their parents to openly discuss SRHR, GBV and GP through playing Ishema Ryanjye cards game and other SRHR games.

URPHSA to together with HPO initiated Ishema Ryanjye Project which is under the program of Adolescents Sexual and Reproductive Health and Rights. Its aim is to address problems of teenage pregnancy and sexual transmitted diseases through comprehensive sexuality education and behavior change communication. Despite all measures put in place either by the government of Rwanda or other organizations to tackle the issue of teenage pregnancies, Muhanga district had the highest rate of teenage pregnancies of 12% and Gasabo district had 6% as evidenced by Rwanda DHS districts profile of 2019-2020.
  • To train and capacitate public health club leaders in secondary schools and Yego centers on comprehensive sexuality education to become peer educators in their clubs.
  • To crack fears of adolescents to openly discuss SRHR, GBV and gender promotion through playing Ishema Ryanjye cards game not only between adolescents themselves but also parents and their children.
  • To increase awareness among adolescents to take control over their life and secure their future than anyone else by working with secondary schools and Yego centers.

MFITE UBUZIMA

Non-Communicable Diseases Program coordinates prevention, early detection, diagnosis and rehabilitation interventions to prevent and mitigate NCDs burden in community. The program aims to protect Rwandans from morbidity and mortality related to Non-Communicable Diseases through integrated prevention and management approaches.

URPHSA to together with HPO initiated KUZA NEZA Project Health Through Promotion Organization NCDs Program works in close collaboration with different government and non-government organizations, institutions and other partners to detect and mitigate the burden of NCDs as well as their management in the community of Rwanda.
  • Increasing awareness in the community about prevention and early detection of Non-Communicable Diseases.
  • Early detection of Non-Communicable Diseases in the community through organized screening campaigns.
  • Developing innovative approaches to prevention and management of Non-Communicable Diseases.
  • Providing comprehensive education on prevention and management of Non-Communicable Diseases using nutrition interventions.
  • Promotion of research and development related to Non-Communicable Diseases.

KUZA NEZA PROJECT

The double burden of malnutrition requires a comprehensive approach to act on it, and that is why nutrition program works in close collaboration with different partners to contribute to reduction of all forms of malnutrition. In this program, we use different approaches to protect maternal and child health in the community and strengthening school feeding program through nutrition sensitive and specific interventions to tackle malnutrition issues.

URPHSA to together with HPO initiated KUZA NEZA Project which is under the program of nutrition program. Its main aims are:
  • Increasing community awareness on healthy nutrition through nutrition education on balanced diet.
  • Teaching communities to wisely choose healthy and nutritious foods while shopping food for cooking.
  • TParticipatory cooking demonstrations to teach caregivers how to prepare a balanced diet at the community.
  • Providing accurate information on food handling, hygiene and sanitation to prevent foodborne illnesses and other related diseases which can lead to malnutrition.
  • Using home grown solutions including establishment of kitchen gardens and planting fruits trees at home and schools to increase access to different micronutrients and instilling the culture of fruits and vegetable consumption by Rwandan community.

ISHEMA RYANJYE PROJECT

Maintaining and enhancing human health requires assessing, correcting, controlling, and preventing individuals from physical, chemical, and biological risks/hazards through the provision of access to clean water, decent toilets, proper hygiene practices and environmental safety.

Our program seeks to provide fair, long-term, and cost-effective access to water and sanitation facilities and environmental safety to Rwandan communities. its main aims are:
  • Building and rehabilitating toilets and hygiene facilities.
  • Rehabilitation of damaged water systems.
  • We provide environmental safety trainings
  • Providing safe drinking water and constructing water treatment tanks.
  • We conduct hygiene promotions and behavior change campaigns activities to improve peopleâ€™s knowledge of good sanitation and hygiene practices (such as proper personal hygiene, home hygiene, food hygiene, kitchen hygiene). Additionally, we assist people in efficiently managing and maintaining their water, sanitation, and hygiene infrastructure.
  • We intervene in diseases prevention and control such as zoonotic diseases (Ebola, rift valley), malaria prevention, infectious, Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).
  • Promoting climate action and environmental conservation, to reduce green house effect through increasing awareness at the community and capacity building to different organizations and people to identify the opportunities that can reduce green house gas emission and voluntary actions that prevent climate change.
